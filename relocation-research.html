1 Quellensteuer (Withholding Tax)

CriticalBudget

B-permit holders pay tax at source. Peter's CHF 180K gross exceeds the CHF 120K threshold, requiring mandatory ordinary taxation. Estimated effective rate: 20-23% on taxable income (~CHF 140-150K after deductions).

  • Estimated monthly tax: CHF 2,300-2,800
  • Currently modeled as flat rate — needs progressive calculation
  • Good news: ordinary taxation enables ALL deductions (3a, BVG buy-in, commuting, child support)

2 UVG Accident Insurance

CriticalBudget

UVG is mandatory but not in the budget model. Occupational accidents: employer-paid. Non-occupational (NBUV): employee pays ~1-2% of insured salary.

  • On CHF 148,200 cap: ~CHF 1,482-2,964/year
  • Monthly deduction: ~CHF 125-250 (varies by insurer)
  • Critical for Peter given knee condition (covers medical costs from any accident)

3 Serafe (Radio/TV Fee)

HighBudget

Mandatory Swiss household fee: CHF 335/year (CHF 28/month). Every Swiss household pays regardless of whether they own a TV/radio. Invoiced quarterly.

4 Pillar 3a Maximum Correction

HighBudget

Current code uses CHF 7,056 (2024 value). The 2026 maximum is CHF 7,258. This affects tax savings calculations too — the deduction is worth ~CHF 1,500/year in saved tax.

Insurance Intelligence

Swiss insurance landscape with specific relevance for Peter's knee condition

♥ KVG (Mandatory Health)

3-Month Deadline
  • Must enroll within 90 days of arrival (by Oct 1, 2026)
  • Basic coverage: CHF 300-500/month depending on insurer & deductible
  • Deductible options: CHF 300 (highest premium) to CHF 2,500 (lowest premium)
  • For Peter's knee: choose CHF 300 deductible — frequent medical visits expected
  • No pre-existing condition exclusions in KVG (mandatory acceptance)
  • Compare: Comparis.ch, Priminfo.admin.ch

+ VVG (Supplementary Health)

High Priority
  • Critical for Peter's knee condition
  • Covers: private/semi-private hospital rooms, free choice of doctor, complementary medicine
  • Insurers CAN reject or add exclusions for pre-existing conditions
  • Apply BEFORE moving if possible (health questionnaire)
  • Typical cost: CHF 100-300/month depending on coverage
  • Key: "Division privée" for hospital stays (own room, chief physician)

⌂ Household & Liability

Medium
  • Privathaftpflicht (personal liability): CHF 5-15/month — covers damages to third parties
  • Hausratversicherung (household contents): CHF 15-30/month
  • Often bundled: CHF 20-40/month combined
  • Many landlords require liability insurance proof
  • Consider: glass breakage, bicycle theft, water damage riders

⚖ Rechtsschutz (Legal Protection)

Medium
  • Covers legal fees for disputes: tenant rights, employment, traffic, insurance claims
  • Relevant for Peter: tenant protection (Swiss rental law is complex), insurance disputes (knee treatment coverage)
  • Cost: CHF 20-40/month
  • Recommended: tenant + health insurance legal coverage

★ UVG (Accident Insurance)

Budget
  • BU (occupational): employer-paid
  • NBU (non-occupational): employee pays ~1-2%
  • Covers: all accidents including sports, travel
  • Medical costs 100% covered (no deductible)
  • Daily allowance: 80% of insured salary
  • Peter's knee: any NEW injury covered; existing condition is not

⚠ Insurance Timeline

Deadlines
  • Before July 1: Apply for VVG supplementary (no pre-existing exclusion risk if done early)
  • July 1: UVG coverage starts automatically via employer
  • By Oct 1: Enroll in KVG basic health insurance (90-day deadline)
  • Before signing lease: Get liability insurance (landlord requirement)
  • After arrival: Compare and purchase household contents insurance

UX Design Audit

Information architecture, cross-module connectivity, and dashboard effectiveness

Overall assessment: QuaiPulse has strong visual design, thoughtful data density, and solid keyboard navigation. The Bloomberg Terminal aesthetic is well-executed. Primary weaknesses: cross-module connectivity (Neighborhoods ↔ Apartments ↔ Budget disconnected), dashboard actionability (shows state not urgency), and 19 nav items overwhelming.

H1 Neighborhoods → Apartments Link

CriticalUX

When viewing a neighborhood (e.g., Wiedikon K3), there's no "See apartments in K3" button. User must manually navigate and set filters. This is THE core workflow.

FIX: Add "View apartments in Kreis X" button on neighborhood detail pages, pre-filtering the Apartments page.

H2 Move Readiness Score

CriticalUX

No single "am I on track?" metric. Need composite score: checklist %, apartment secured, budget finalized, documents ready. "Move Readiness: 42%" ring chart on dashboard.

FIX: Compute composite readiness score and show as progress ring on dashboard.

H3 Upcoming Deadlines Widget

CriticalUX

Dashboard shows state but not urgency. No "Next 3 deadlines" or "Overdue tasks" widget. A 49-year-old managing a complex move needs action-oriented prompts.

H4 Unify Rental Intel & Apartments

CriticalUX

Two parallel apartment-tracking systems with no connection. Saving from Rental Intel's top picks doesn't add to Apartments pipeline. Merge or add save-to-pipeline flow.

M1 Replace Static "Mission" KPI

HighUX

"Peter Blazsik / Finance AI & Innovation Lead" is static and wastes premium real estate. Replace with current relocation phase or next critical action.

M2 Apartment Pipeline on Dashboard

HighUX

Highest-stakes activity has zero dashboard presence. Show: "3 interested, 1 viewing, 1 applied" with link to Apartments page.

M4 Inline Budget Impact on Apartments

HighUX

When viewing a CHF 2,400 apartment, show "This rent would leave you CHF X surplus" inline. Closes the cognitive gap between apartment evaluation and budget impact.

M6 Next Katie Visit Date

HighUX

Katie card shows "X visits planned" but not WHEN. The next visit date is far more actionable than the total count. Show date + days-until.

Accessibility & Mobile

Key gaps identified in the UX audit

Checklist Checkbox Semantics

Critical

Checklist uses <button> not <input type="checkbox">. Missing role="checkbox" and aria-checked. Screen readers can't determine checked state.

Skip-to-Content Link

High

No skip link. Keyboard users must tab through 19+ sidebar items to reach content. Add hidden skip-to-content link in layout.

Color-Only Indicators

Medium

Apartment pipeline status and value scores use color-only encoding (red/amber/green). Color-blind users can't distinguish. Add text labels or pattern differentiation.

Mobile Bottom Navigation

Low

No bottom tab bar. One-handed mobile use requires reaching top-left hamburger. Add bottom tabs for 5 most-used modules: Dashboard, Neighborhoods, Apartments, Budget, Checklist.
